> Every program eventually expands to extracting your data for the benefit of its proprietors, apps that don't are replaced by those who do.

~ My addendum to Zawinski's Law

Hubris is excessive pride an arrogance, originally to the point that the individual believed they were equal to or better than the gods; and never has this been more clear in the Programmer Personality Type[^1] than it is today.
We have a shiny new god, Generative AI, and it will solve all the world's problems, once we figure out how to make it figure out what problems actually are.

This is behind all art and writing of humanity being flattened to "content," the programmers made the platform, they don't know or care what content will go on it, only that the platform itself displays the content in the intended way. What is the intended way? The programmer doesn't know, so they've made "Intelligent Guesses" sometimes unalterable intelligent guesses[^2].
The digital archives contain less than 1% of the world's written works, according to the University of Michigan
Maybe the intelligent guesses about layout are made by a professional designer, but the devs don't need that, they've developed "Iterative A/B Testing" where they can show the same content in different layouts and formats to different people to see how those people react.

Nobody "Important" cares about writing software or writing good software; they only care about selling it and not being asked for refunds, there's no use in optimizing the software anyhow &mdash; Disk space and Memory aren't considerations for the end-user, just so long as it works on your machine it should be fine.
