The internet breeds extremism. It's the perfect place for it. You sit safely in
your home, away from prying eyes, alone with every other person secreted away
and trawling the internet. It's easy to find an echo chamber that's more than
happy to turn you into One of Them, and There's nobody to stop you from
learning about what you want to know, except for your own limitations (or some
DMCA notice that only slows you down), and you don't know what you don't know
and probably can't imagine it from the first principles you already know.

http://www.cracked.com/blog/5-reasons-internet-always-prefers-revenge-over-justice/

Internet algorithms are bubbling you, learning what you like, think, agree
with, and then repeating the same sort of content to you over and over; no
opposing views, no Real Facts&trade;, just the things YOU want to see. It
generates In-Groups and Out-Groups from the Demographic marketing data, every
little thing is a signifier, another facet in what and who these databases
think you are.

Twitter[^x] has pitchforks and torches prepared for your convenience. Social
Media has become an open-world Player-vs-Player experience, where the algorithm
enhances the powers of those most experienced at cruelty. The Spectacle of
Bloodsport remains, just in an ideological fantasy world of our collective
imagination.
