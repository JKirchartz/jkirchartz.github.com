---
title: Attrition
---

It's not just Employee Turnover; it's the ability to wear away something
through abrasion, to grind it down into nothingness.  Sometimes this is just
normal wear and tear, replacing the tools that have been worn down to
uselessness with a fresh piece of metal that still has an edge.  Attrition is
the cost of doing business; in a CNC Tooling Company the attrition happens to
the actual tools being used, Carbide and Steel have different costs, different
longevity, and are equally in need of being replaced. After cutting through
metal like butter for a while they begin to behave more like a slice of bread
being cut through by cold butter. The cutting edge has been used up and needs
to be replaced to continue working as it had before.

Attrition warfare is a military strategy to gradually wear down opponents to
the point of collapse by inflicting continuous losses in personnel/moral and
resources. But what do you call it when you're doing to to your own employees?
Increasing shareholder value? Maybe they're not your own employees, maybe
they're contractors or sub-contractors; what happens to these people when you
throw them away? Are they loyal and valued customers after being ground into a
fine powder by your processes?
