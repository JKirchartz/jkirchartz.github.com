They say that the more things change that the more they stay the same, but,
well, Computing has gotten a little passe.

It used to be exciting seeing all the new, the possibilities were endless, and
Hackers were prying interesting technologies from corporations cold claws. But
a hacker's got to eat, too. And there was still money to be made, but cut out
from the side of Enterprise Business, to create a Consumer Market; but now the
consumers are being priced out of the Computer market-- fortunately our phones
have more than enough compute for most people -- but all the Real Money is in
B2B.

These enterprises have put computer power, voice recognition, and AI into
consumer devices that never needed them; they didn't even bother to secure the
devices. These Tech Startups move fast and break things, but when your
Internet-Connected device's servers get shut down, they break your devices too.
There's no real warranty, nothing lasts forever, Caveat Emptor, etc.


> If you can't dazzle them with your brilliance, blind them with bullshit
> -- Ancient Con-Artist Proverb (popularly attributed to W.C. Fields)
